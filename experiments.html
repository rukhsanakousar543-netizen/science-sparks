50 Fun & Easy Science Experiments

Each experiment is simple, safe and explained in one short paragraph.

1. Vinegar Volcano

Put baking soda in a small container, add vinegar and food color — the CO₂ bubbles cause a bubbly “eruption.”

2. Floating Egg

Fill a glass with water and add salt gradually; when density increases the egg begins to float.

3. Dancing Raisins

Drop raisins into fizzy soda; bubbles attach to them and lift them up, then when bubbles pop raisins sink.

4. Balloon Rocket

Thread a straw on a string, tape a blown-up balloon to the straw, release the air — it zips along the string.

5. Invisible Ink

Write with lemon juice on paper; heat gently and the writing appears as the juice oxidizes and browns.

6. Paper Towel Rainbow

Place colored water in jars and connect them with paper towel strips — capillary action pulls water and mixes colors.

7. Rainbow Milk

Drop food coloring on milk and add a little dish soap — the colors move because soap changes surface tension.

8. Walking Water

Place cups with colored water and a dry cup between them; paper towels carry water from cup to cup over time.

9. Skittle Color Spread

Arrange Skittles in a circle on a plate, pour warm water — the sugar colors spread and form a circle pattern.

10. Pepper Repellent

Sprinkle pepper on water; touch soap-covered finger to surface — pepper scatters since soap breaks surface tension.

11. Static Balloon

Rub a balloon on hair to charge it; it can pick up small paper bits or stick to a wall.

12. Water Bending

Charge a comb by rubbing and bring it near a thin stream of water — the stream bends toward the comb.

13. Lava Lamp

Fill a jar with oil, water and food color, then drop fizz tablets — bubbles move like a lava lamp.

14. Balloon vs. Fire

Fill a balloon with water and hold it over a small flame — the water absorbs heat so the balloon doesn't pop (careful & adult supervision!).

15. Ice Fishing

Place string on ice, sprinkle salt then press string — salt melts then refreezes so the string sticks and “fishes” a piece of ice.

16. Cloud in a Jar

Warm water in jar, add a little smoke or hairspray then cold lid/ice — you can see a tiny cloud form.

17. Magnetic Slime

Add iron filings (or magnetic powder) to slime to make it respond to magnets (use caution with small kids).

18. Oobleck

Mix cornstarch & water — the result is a non-Newtonian fluid that behaves like a solid under sudden force and like a liquid slowly.

19. Lemon Battery

Insert a zinc nail and copper coin into a lemon to create a small voltage — connect multiple for more power.

20. DIY Compass

Stroke a needle with a magnet and float it on a small piece of cork in water — it aligns with Earth's magnetic field.

21. Bottle Tornado

Fill a bottle with water, flip it and swirl — a vortex forms like a tornado.

22. Seed Germination

Place seeds between damp paper towels inside a zip bag and watch roots and sprouts appear in a few days.

23. Candle Vacuum

Light a candle then place a glass over it; as oxygen is used, water rises into the glass from a tray (pressure change).

24. Egg in a Bottle

Light a small paper inside a bottle to heat the air, place boiled egg on the mouth — cooling air pressure pushes the egg in.

25. Melting Ice Race

Test different substances (salt, sugar, sand) on ice to compare melting speeds.

26. Sun Water Purifier

Create a simple solar still: evaporate water into a clean container using sunlight and condensation.

27. Shadow Measuring

Use a stick and its shadow at noon to learn about the sun's angle and measure lengths.

28. Balloon Static Motor

Create a static charge and use it to turn a small paper wheel or spinner.

29. Water Cycle in a Bag

Place water in a sealed plastic bag in sunlight — observe evaporation and condensation inside the bag.

30. Rubber Band Guitar

Stretch rubber bands across a box to make a simple string instrument and explore pitch changes.

31. Air Pressure Can Crush

Heat a little water in a can, invert it into cold water — the can is crushed as pressure drops.

32. Homemade Barometer

Stretch balloon over a jar and note how the balloon surface moves up/down as air pressure changes.

33. Sugar Density Tower

Layer sugar-water solutions of different concentrations to make colorful density layers.

34. Fireproof Balloon

Fill balloon with a little water; hold over flame — water protects rubber from heat (adult supervision!).

35. Floating Paper Clip

Float a paper clip on water by carefully placing it on the surface and using surface tension.

36. Capillary Flowers

Fold coffee filter into flower, put petals in colored water — watch them absorb color and 'open'.

37. Balloon Air Pump

Mix vinegar and baking soda inside a bottle and attach a balloon to capture the released gas.

38. Rain Cloud Jar

Fill jar with water, add shaving foam as cloud and drop colored water — the colored water falls like rain.

39. Mirror Fog

Breathe onto a mirror to make it fog; watch how droplets form and run — condensation demonstration.

40. Static Water Bend

Charge a balloon and use it to bend a thin stream of running water by attraction of opposite charges.

41. Mini Catapult

Build a simple catapult with popsicle sticks and rubber bands to study stored elastic energy.

42. pH Test with Red Cabbage

Make cabbage indicator juice — it changes color with acids and bases.

43. Floating Oranges

Compare peeled vs unpeeled orange in water — peel changes overall density.

44. Bottle Rocket

Use water and air pressure in a plastic bottle to launch like a rocket (adult supervision required).

45. Salt Water Freeze Test

Freeze salty vs fresh water and compare freezing points and textures.

46. Chromatography Art

Use coffee filters and markers to separate ink colors with a bit of water.

47. Orange Peel Candle

Fill an orange peel with oil and light the center — it behaves like a small candle (careful & supervised).

48. Pendulum Waves

Hang multiple pendulums of different lengths to create wave patterns and beats.

49. Sun-Melting Crayons

Arrange crayons on paper and leave in sun to create melted crayon art (do on safe surface).

50. Ice Cream in a Bag

Shake cream + sugar + vanilla in a bag with ice + salt to freeze quickly into ice cream.
